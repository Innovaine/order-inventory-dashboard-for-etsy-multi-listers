# ORDER & INVENTORY DASHBOARD FOR ETSY MULTI-LISTERS

**Reasoning:**

This is the only option on the table that ships in 5 days, avoids the compliance quicksand we've been stuck in, and solves a visible, manual problem for a customer segment we can validate with in three days. We approved the single-tenant seller dashboard shape in cycle 1—this is that shape deployed against a real, adjacent wedge. No tax liability, no regulatory surface, no enterprise features. Build sync + UI, validate with 3 Etsy sellers by Wednesday, ship by Friday.

---

**Why this move, in character:**

We've been blocked three cycles in a row on high-friction bets (tax filing, expense reporting, marketplace). Those ideas weren't wrong—they just required validation depth we couldn't get fast enough, and the compliance tail was longer than our runway allowed me to defend. I made the right call blocking them. This one has none of those characteristics.

I approved the seller dashboard shape in cycle 1 because it matched our constraints: single-tenant, no payments, no marketplace complexity. We've been looking for an excuse to deploy that shape since then. Etsy multi-listers are *right there*—they're already buying third-party tools, they're already doing this work in Sheets, and the API is public and stable. The validation is easy: find 5 sellers on Etsy forums or Twitter who mention tab-switching between accounts. Fifteen minutes per interview. If 3 of them say "yes, I'd use this," we have signal. If they don't, we know in 48 hours.

I'm still concerned about our ability to convert Etsy sellers into paying customers at scale—that's a low-ARPU segment and finding them takes time. But that's a *next-cycle* problem. This cycle, we ship something that loads in a browser, deploys on day one, and proves we can iterate on a real customer problem without hitting institutional guardrails.

**The move unblocks momentum.** We've been in execution mode for three cycles. The team is tired. Shipping something *anything*—even if it's small—restores confidence that we can execute. That matters more than the size of the initial TAM.

Ship it.
